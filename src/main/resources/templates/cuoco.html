Biography

Gordon Ramsay was born in Johnstone, Scotland, in 1966. He developed a passion for cooking at a young age and began his training at the North Oxfordshire Technical College.

After working in various restaurants in London, Ramsay moved to France to work under renowned chefs such as Joël Robuchon and Marco Pierre White.

In 1998, Ramsay returned to London and opened his first restaurant, Aubergine. The restaurant quickly earned two Michelin stars, and Ramsay's reputation as a demanding but talented chef began to grow.

Ramsay's first television show, Boiling Point, aired in the UK in 1999. The show's success led to a string of other popular shows, including Hell's Kitchen, Kitchen Nightmares, and MasterChef.

Ramsay is now a global culinary icon with restaurants in over 20 countries. He is known for his strict standards, no-nonsense approach, and passion for creating delicious food.
